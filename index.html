The story in one sentence

2025 was about turning institutional communication into visible, measurable and shareable impact.

The app now follows a vertical narrative: each scroll reveals one communication layer, supported by a strong image, one main message and one evidence point.

Chapter 1 · Media visibility

A record year for media coverage.

28,281 media mentions made 2025 the strongest year since 2017. Visibility was driven by campaign narratives, plenaries, EURegionsWeek and political moments that travelled beyond Brussels.

0media mentions

Chapter 2 · Social media

Growth came from formats that people actually use.

The CoR reached 261,307 followers across social channels. Organic engagements more than doubled, helped by reels, stories, collaborations and content diversification.

Chapter 3 · Audiovisual

Political content became screen-ready.

Recordings, studio debates, clips and podcasts transformed institutional messages into reusable formats for media, social platforms and web audiences.

Chapter 4 · Photo service

Images helped the story travel.

Photo production increased strongly, while Flickr views reached 4.2 million.

Chapter 5 · Events

From rooms to regions, participation remained central.

137 events brought together around 27,000 participants, connecting institutional priorities with regional and local audiences.

Chapter 6 · Campaigns

Four narratives structured the year.

Proximity, Cohesion, Resilience and Ukraine combined media, social media, visual production, events and digital channels.

Chapter 7 · Digital and innovation

More interactive, more accessible storytelling.

Website growth, webstreaming, Dynamics journeys, visual identities and emerging AI-driven tools point towards more personalised and data-informed communication.

Conclusion

Communication works when impact is visible.

The 2025 results confirm the value of combining political clarity, strong visuals, reliable data, accessible journeys and formats designed for modern audiences.
